INTERNATIONAL DAY OF PLAY

International Day of Play is a reminder that play is not just for children. It is something people need at every stage of life.

As routines and responsibilities take over, play is often pushed aside. This day brings the focus back to making space for movement, freedom, and simple moments of enjoyment.

Why it matters

Play has a direct impact on wellbeing. Adults who engage in regular play report lower stress, better mood, and stronger social connections.

It also supports creativity and flexible thinking by allowing the mind to reset without pressure. Physically, active play contributes to overall health and helps reduce the effects of a sedentary lifestyle.

Play is not something extra. It is part of what keeps people balanced and functioning at their best.
